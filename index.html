04 · The plan TMGM · Retail Acquisition · H2 Recovery Plan

Four moves close the gap — deploy, offer, retarget, track.

Deploy the idle budget for deposits, put one sharp offer to the verified base, pay only to chase the warm ones who don't bite, and fix tracking so every dollar is provable.

More on Move 1
$2.6M The ad budget we have but haven't spent — ~$3.0M for the year, only ~$0.4M spent, so ~$2.6M is idle. Move 1 deploys it.
The situation

We have about $3 million this year for ads to bring in new paying customers, and we've spent only about $400k — the rest is sitting unused.

The move

Spend it — aimed at deposits, not just sign-ups.

How we do it

Buy more sign-ups and you get people who register and never put money in — that's what happened earlier this year. So we point the money at people who actually deposit:

  • Optimise for the deposit event — tell each platform (Google, Meta, Moloco) to chase deposits, not sign-ups: send the deposit back as its goal, and target people who resemble our real depositors
  • Follow the conversions — rank channels by cost per deposit, not per click; move budget to the best and cap the rest (Google converts well above our blended average)
  • Both need Move 4 — deposits must be tracked back to each channel first, or none of this works
Setting it up · technical
  • Store the click ID at sign-up — Meta fbclid, Google gclid, Moloco device/click ID — saved against each lead in Salesforce (no ID stored, no way to tie a later deposit to the ad)
  • Fire the deposit server-side when the FTD lands (it happens post-KYC, often days later, so browser pixels miss it): Meta CAPI, Google offline conversion import via gclid, Moloco server postback; app deposits flow through AppsFlyer
  • Switch each campaign's goal from registration to that deposit event; pass the deposit amount to unlock value-based bidding (Meta value opt., Google tROAS) once volume allows
  • Bridge low volume — algorithms need ~30–50 of the event per ad set per week; until deposits are that frequent, optimise on KYC-complete as a proxy, then switch
  • Build audiences from depositors — hashed list → Meta Custom Audience / Google Customer Match / Moloco seed → lookalikes; exclude existing depositors (they go to Move 2)
  • Weekly cost-per-deposit report per channel = spend ÷ FTDs (this is Move 3): below target → scale, above → cap or cut
How we pace it

Channels warm up at different speeds:

  • Now — scale what's already working
  • September — Google was switched off; spend too fast and it can get blocked, so we build up slowly
  • Oct–Dec — the Middle East awareness ads (via Moloco) take time to turn into deposits
The money already exists — we just need to spend it on deposits, at a pace that works.

More on Move 2
~1,000 Deposits we can win back from people we already have — 20,000+ verified but never funded. A directional target, not a promise; even a few hundred funded traders is close to free FTDs.
The situation

More than 20,000 people signed up and passed our ID check — then never put any money in. We already paid to bring them here — and their blocker usually isn't the deposit, it's the fear of losing on a first trade.

The move

One month, one offer: 100% First Time Deposit Cashback + 100% First Trade Cashback — one per KYC'd ID.

Why it's the cheapest win

We've already paid to find and verify these people, so getting them to deposit costs almost nothing extra — no new prospects to buy. Winning back just 1 in 20 is about 1,000 deposits, roughly an eighth of what we need this half.

The mechanic · three tiers
  • Tier 1 · First Time Deposit Cashback — 100% of the first deposit only (top-ups don't count), capped at $50, no minimum — deposit $5 get $5, deposit $50+ get $50
  • Tier 2 · First Trade Cashback — trade 1 lot to unlock (a small first deposit tops up to get there); if that first trade loses, we refund the loss 100%, capped at $50 — a winning first trade keeps its profit, so no rebate needed. Both cashbacks then pay as real, withdrawable cash — manually distributed at campaign end. No trade, no payout (Tier 1 releases only when Tier 2 is done)
  • Tier 3 · Lucky draw — every Tier-2 completer is entered; 1 lot traded = 1 entry, capped at 10; grand prize is an air ticket — requires net deposit held 60 days (no withdrawal). 1 winner.
Why it converts — and stays safe
  • The funnel is the engine — deposit small → top up → trade 1 lot → unlock; the low entry breaks the fear, the lot requirement pulls in real money and real trading
  • The gates — nothing pays until deposited + traded + campaign ended; one per KYC'd ID caps exposure at $100/person; a short hold or turnover before Tier 2 releases keeps it clean
  • Up to $100 per converter (Tier 1 + Tier 2, each up to $50) — roughly on par with the ~$102 per FTD today, but for an already-acquired, now actively-trading customer (average lands lower, as Tier 1 tracks small first deposits)
  • Delivered free — email · SMS · app push; Move 3 pays only to chase the warm ones who don't bite
Setting it up · technical
  • Build the segment in Salesforce — filter KYC-complete AND no FTD, with recency, source and geo fields; that's the master win-back list
  • Sync to owned channels — CRM / automation flows for email · SMS · app push; paid audiences are Move 3's job
  • Wire the tiers — promo engine tracking first-deposit basis · 1-lot unlock · $50 caps · cash payout at campaign end; manual payout checklist (deposited ✓ · traded 1 lot ✓ · one per ID ✓ · not flagged ✓)
  • Tag the cohort — so deposits from this list attribute back to the campaign (via Move 4's tracking), letting you measure the ~1,000 FTDs
  • Suppress on success — auto-remove anyone who deposits from the flow; exclude flagged abusers
  • Compliance gate — deposit promos are restricted in some markets; the final mechanic is geo-screened before launch
Like a shop with thousands of people who walked in, looked around, and left — the cheapest sales come from bringing them back, not finding new visitors.

More on Move 3
Warm We only pay to chase warm leads — people who opened, clicked, or started a deposit off Move 2 but didn't fund. Never the whole list, never cold.
The situation

Even a strong offer converts a minority on the first touch. Email and push are free but easy to ignore — the ones who leaned in and stalled need a nudge somewhere they'll actually see it.

The move

Turn Move 2's engagement into paid audiences, and follow up on Meta and Google with the same offer.

How we do it
  • Segment by heat — started-a-deposit first, then clickers, then openers; the closer they got, the harder we chase
  • Same offer, same window — the ads remind, they don't introduce something new
  • Exclude ruthlessly — anyone who deposits drops out daily; cold non-engagers stay on free channels only
  • Cap frequency — a nudge, not a stalker — this is an audience we already own
Setting it up · technical
  • Pipe engagement events — opens · clicks · deposit-page visits from the campaign into audience lists
  • Sync to platforms — hashed lists → Meta Custom Audience + Google Customer Match; site visitors via the remarketing tag
  • Daily exclusions — converters and flagged accounts drop out automatically
  • Attribute back — tag these campaigns so their FTDs credit to the win-back cohort (via Move 4)
Free channels make the offer; ad money only chases the ones who leaned in — the cheapest possible use of paid media.

More on Move 4
1 of 61 How blind we are today — of 61 channel spend lines, only 1 has a deposit number attached; for the other 60 we see spend but not results. Move 4 fixes that.
The situation

We run ads on many platforms, but our sheet only records how much we spend on each — not how many paying customers each one brings in. We can't see what actually works.

The move

Put a counter on each channel — link every ad platform to the customers it produces, and report it weekly.

Why it matters

Without this, "spend more on what works" is a guess — we can't see what works. With it, every dollar goes where it actually brings in customers — it's what lets Move 1 aim at deposits and proves what Moves 2 and 3 deliver.

How we do it
  • Tag every channel consistently — one naming convention plus tracking parameters on every campaign, so a deposit can always be traced back to what brought it
  • Stitch source → deposit in the CRM — connect each FTD back to the channel that first brought the lead, in Salesforce
  • Publish a weekly channel report — spend ÷ FTDs per channel = cost-per-deposit, ranked best to worst
  • Act on it — reallocate budget each week toward the lowest cost-per-deposit; that's what makes Moves 1, 2 and 3 provable
Setting it up · technical
  • Naming + UTM schema — a fixed campaign convention and source / medium / campaign tags across every platform, so "channel" is machine-readable
  • Capture source at sign-up — store the click ID / UTM / referrer against each lead in Salesforce (shared with Move 1's tracking)
  • Attribution model — pick first- or last-touch, then stitch lead → KYC → FTD so each deposit maps to exactly one channel
  • Automate the report — pull spend per channel and FTDs per channel weekly, compute cost-per-deposit, output to a dashboard
  • Data-team owned — needs data-team time to build, and a set weekly cadence to keep it live
Like running ten billboards but only knowing what each one costs — not which one actually brings people through the door.
Move 4
Fix the blind spot
We track spend by channel but not FTDs by channel — so "scale the winners" is guesswork today. Build channel-level tracking so every deployment decision runs on data, not hope.
